The (fictional) Cereal Health Research Group studies plant–pathogen interactions in staple cereal crops, with a focus on foliar fungal disease in sorghum across smallholder farming systems in sub-Saharan Africa. The group combines greenhouse pathogen-response trials, multi-site field surveillance, and diagnostic-tool development to understand how disease pressure varies with climate, host genotype, and management practice.

The group's current flagship effort is a multi-year surveillance and trial programme on sorghum leaf blight (a fictional/composite disease system used for this workshop), spanning three trial sites with contrasting rainfall and soil conditions. Work spans controlled inoculation trials, on-farm disease scoring, and early-stage evaluation of a biocontrol seed treatment as a lower-cost alternative to fungicide programmes for resource-constrained farmers.

The group works with national agricultural research partners, extension services, and participating farmer cooperatives to ground findings in real field conditions, and places strong emphasis on open, reproducible methods so results can be independently verified and extended by other groups working on related pathosystems.
