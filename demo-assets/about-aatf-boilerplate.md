The African Agricultural Technology Foundation (AATF) is an African-led, not-for-profit organisation that facilitates the transfer and delivery of agricultural technologies to smallholder farmers across Sub-Saharan Africa. Founded in 2003 and headquartered in Nairobi, Kenya, AATF works at the intersection of science, policy, and the market to put practical, affordable technology solutions into the hands of the farmers who need them most.

AATF's vision is a prosperous, food- and nutrition-secure Africa. Its mission is to broker partnerships and manage the access, stewardship, and deployment of proven agricultural innovations — improved seed, crop-protection technologies, and complementary agronomic practices — so that resource-constrained farmers can raise productivity, build resilience to climate stress, and improve their livelihoods.

Working with public research institutes, private technology developers, national governments, seed companies, and farmer organisations, AATF negotiates royalty-free or affordable access to technologies and coordinates the regulatory, stewardship, and commercialisation steps required to reach farmers at scale. Flagship programme areas span drought-tolerant and insect-protected maize, pest-resistant cowpea, cassava improvement, and integrated Striga weed control, among others.

The Foundation places strong emphasis on responsible stewardship, gender-inclusive delivery, and building the capacity of local partners so that impact is durable and African-owned. By connecting world-class innovation with last-mile delivery systems, AATF aims to help millions of smallholder households move from subsistence toward sustainable, market-oriented farming.
