# Reading List Summary — Sorghum Leaf Blight

**FICTIONAL DEMO DATA.** The five entries below are invented for workshop use — deliberately labelled "(fictional)" so no one mistakes them for real literature to cite. They exist only so a demo "Literature Assistant" Project has something to synthesise from.

---

**Study A (fictional, greenhouse).** Inoculated seedlings across 12 genotypes; found a roughly three-fold range in lesion size, with the most tolerant genotypes clustering in one breeding lineage. Did not test field performance.

**Study B (fictional, multi-site field survey).** Surveyed disease severity across two seasons and found severity tracked cumulative rainfall in the four weeks after emergence more closely than total seasonal rainfall — a timing effect, not just an amount effect.

**Study C (fictional, biocontrol trial).** Small on-farm trial of a seed-applied biocontrol treatment reported a reduction in severity versus untreated controls, but the trial was underpowered (n=3 farms per arm) and not replicated across sites.

**Study D (fictional, review).** A narrative review arguing that most existing severity-scoring scales in this pathosystem are not well validated against yield loss, and calling for standardised, yield-linked scoring.

**Study E (fictional, genomics).** Identified several candidate resistance loci in a mapping population, but the study flagged that effect sizes were inconsistent across the two environments tested.

## What this suggests (for synthesis demos)

- **Established:** genotype matters (A, E); disease severity is climate-timing-sensitive, not just climate-amount-sensitive (B).
- **Contested:** how much a biocontrol seed treatment actually helps at scale — the one trial (C) is too small to generalise from.
- **Missing:** a validated, yield-linked severity scale (D) and multi-site confirmation of the candidate resistance loci (E).
