# Donor Profile — Donor Foundation X

**FICTIONAL DEMO DATA — not real AATF information.** "Donor Foundation X" is an invented funder for workshop use. Any resemblance to a real foundation is coincidental.

---

**Overview:** Donor Foundation X is a mid-sized private philanthropy focused on climate-resilient agriculture and rural livelihoods in Sub-Saharan Africa. It favours organisations that can demonstrate a clear pathway from research to farmer-level impact.

**Funding themes:**
- Climate adaptation and drought resilience for smallholder crops
- Inclusive seed systems and last-mile input delivery
- Women's and youth economic participation in agriculture
- Data-driven monitoring and evidence of scale

**Typical grant size:** USD 250,000–750,000 over 24–36 months. Occasional larger "scale" grants up to USD 2 million for proven models.

**Geographic focus:** Priority countries are Kenya, Nigeria, Tanzania, and Ghana. Proposals covering other Sub-Saharan countries are considered only if paired with a priority-country anchor.

**Application quirks:**
- Concept notes must be **two pages maximum** and lead with a single measurable "impact hypothesis."
- The Foundation explicitly asks for a **gender-disaggregated results table** and will decline notes that omit it.
- It dislikes jargon and requires a plain-language summary written "for a non-specialist board member."
- Co-financing of at least 20% is strongly preferred.
- Submissions are by invitation after an initial one-paragraph enquiry; unsolicited full proposals are not reviewed.
