The Ten Simple Rules series

Four published, open-access PLOS Computational Biology papers this workshop draws on: Malik et al. (2020) on engaging with AI in biomedicine; Seckel, Stephens & Rodriguez (2024) on LLMs for grant writing; Crilly et al. (2025) on navigating AI in science; Helmy et al. (2025) on careful generative-AI use. Ask your facilitator for copies.

Keep learning

Follow 2–3 AI-in-science newsletters, not the firehose. Revisit your institution's data-handling policy (e.g. POPIA) as the tools change under you.

A habit, not a one-off
The four ideas that outlast every tool

1. Context is everything.   2. Role · Task · Context · Format.   3. Climb the ladder: Chat → Projects → Cowork → Agents.   4. Verify against the source — every time, whether a human or an AI produced it.
